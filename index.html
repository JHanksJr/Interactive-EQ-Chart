Frequency Range Characteristics

Sub-Bass (20-60 Hz)

Power and rumble. Felt more than heard. Too much = muddy mix. Cut here to clean up the low end.

Bass (60-250 Hz)

Warmth and fullness. Foundation of the mix. Too much = boomy, not enough = thin.

Low Mids (250-500 Hz)

Body and warmth. Can sound muddy if too much. Often cut to add clarity.

Mids (500 Hz-2 kHz)

Core of most instruments. Too much = boxy/nasal. Essential for vocal intelligibility.

High Mids (2-4 kHz)

Presence and definition. Boost for clarity, cut if harsh or aggressive.

Presence (4-8 kHz)

Clarity and attack. Makes vocals cut through. Too much = sibilance and fatigue.

Brilliance (8-20 kHz)

Air and sparkle. Adds shine and openness. Too much = harsh and brittle.

Mixing Tips for Students

  • Start with subtractive EQ (cutting) before additive EQ (boosting)
  • Use high-pass filters to remove unnecessary low frequencies from non-bass instruments
  • Cut narrow, boost wide - surgical cuts for problems, gentle boosts for enhancement
  • Solo to find problem frequencies, but make final decisions in context with the full mix
  • Each instrument doesn't need to occupy the full frequency spectrum
  • Create space by carving out frequencies for different instruments
  • Be careful with 2-4 kHz - this is where ear fatigue happens
  • Less is more - subtle changes often work better than dramatic ones
  • Always A/B compare your changes with the original
  • Take breaks - your ears get tired and need rest for accurate decisions
